February 12, 2018 - February 19th, 2018

For the past week, Alex concerned himself with selecting a web services platform to publish a database on. After extensive research into AWS, Azure, and GoogleCloud, Alex found that AWS offered the best set of features. AWS offers the option for a configurable VPN around the database, which could be important down the line since this project incorporates government data, and the customer placed early emphasis on security. Furthermore, the group agreed that it would be easiest to go ahead and build the database on a company account, instead of using Azure's service because it's free to UK students, but having to transfer the database over to DeltaV at the end of the semester. AWS offered the best free-tier package, and appears to scale database pricing in the fairest way across competition.

Paying mind to logistics, Alex also set up the meeting time and location with the customer, reserving a study room in William T. Young library so that the students could all be together on the video call with the customer.

Finally, Alex collaborated with Dillon to add the necessary updates to the website. He spent some good time breaking down the rest of the semester into manageable chunks of work that the group could accomplish each week, and Dillon got that list published in calendar format to the website.
